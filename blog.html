13/05/24

Stakeholder Interviews

When creating a new product or service the most important thing is to determine, who am I serving?

Who will be affected or impacted by my product?

Who is going to pay me for my work? What type of work are they willing to pay for?

Who is the beneficiary, is it the same person who is paying, or is it someone else?

To answer these questions, we're working from the EcoSystem in towards our target customers. People have been very generous with their time and insights these last 2 months:

* Dept of Finance explained key concepts underpinning 'Financial Literacy in Ireland: Evidence base for a national strategy' & associated action plan to be devloped later this year

* Carlow CCDP: key insights into 'whole-of'community' digital and financial literacy challenges

* Sen Pat Casey: Explained how, during 2024, he toured his constituency equipped with an Excel spreadsheet and a forensic understanding of the 3 homebuying schemes and how they might be combined

* Sorcha Mulligan, mentor and Venture Partner, tech and people enabler

* Susan Hayes Culleton: Shared her observations of promoting youth & female financial literacy across 3 continents

* Leena Kaloinen who activated her network of female financially astute friends to highlight their top concerns

* Caitlin Hafer of Hedgehog financial who provided 'at the front' insights from her financial app

* Techstars Weekend Startup Women organisers, mentors and judges

* Rethink Ireland, for a place on the Social Entrepreneur programme 2024

This is an ever-growing list, we absolutely welcome any feedback or suggestions at this point. We are considering which features might add value within FLIT: discussion is much cheaper than software/ content development!

Feel free to ask difficult questions. I've found that putting an idea out there and letting it get a good kicking, leads to workable solutions with a strong story.

07/05/24

Got funded for feasibility study

Tuesday was a good-news day. I received word that my local enterprise office (LEO) will part-fund a FLIT feasibility study.

This works on a matched-funding basis, where I pay upfront, and once the feasibility study is completed, I can reclaim half the costs under authorised categories.

I aimed high with the costings, and didn't get everything I'd asked for, but it feels like validation and support.

One key recommendation is to produce a marketing report.

Other activities planned for feasibility study are:

* Identify stakeholders: people who would use FLIT, who would pay for FLIT, who would benefit from FLIT's existence.

* Craft detailed customer persona(s) for site users - narrow reach which can later broaden.

* What features are valuable to customers - would financial guidance targeted by lifestage/ decision point be useful? Maybe the ability to create & track personalised task lists? Simplifying processes which require several visits to public websites? A financial healthcheck? Money-confidence building tools? A fraud-recognition game?

* Develop FLIT V1 based on the above

22/03/24

TechStars Startup Weekend Women - 2nd prize!

FLIT.ie attended the pre-natal ward at Techstars Weekend Startup Women 2024.

This is a mini-accelerator(?) where bright spark entrepreneurs pitch an idea on Friday evening, and form a team of 4-7 people to shape the idea, with intense mentoring and masterclasses provided by TechStars, over the 48-hour period.

I brought my rickety MVP and concept to this group, and emerged with a well-formed FLIT business concept, clearly aligned with the SDG goals.

Subjecting an idea to intense scrutiny (particularly when the founder proposes a non-profit-extracting model) meant that FLIT was not only born, it was baptised (in fire).

The weekend culminated with a competitve pitch; in a wonderful communion of minds, the judges awarded FLIT 2nd place.

All that remains is to confirm FLIT via a feasibility study.

The experience and prizes opened doors for FLIT:

* One months co-working space in the Guinnesss Enterprise Centre, led to meeting with Enterprise Ireland & DCC LEO, which in turn led to meeting with The Positive Economist, Susan Hayes Culleton, and joining the Fundr community

* A ticket to Dublin Tech Summit

* Tickets to SaaStock, Nov 2024
