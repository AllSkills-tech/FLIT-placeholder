Why now? to focus on female financial literacy in Ireland?

PESTLE - Political Economic Social Technological Legal and Environmental influences, 2024

Why is now a good time to look at providing a Financial Literacy education system directed at women in Ireland? A number of legal changes, social and economic changes mean that women are increasingly recognised as an interesting market opportunity

Political Economic Social Technical Legal and Environmental (PESTLE) survey reveals large impacts on womens financial lives:

Type Influence Impact
L/E Gender Pay Gap Reporting Anticipate to narrow the gap between male & female income in large industry
L/S WFH Work From Home: Potentially reduces childcare barrier for women returning to work after 2nd child
L/E Auto-enrollment Pensions Auto-enrollment reduces the likelihood of lower wage-earners relying solely on state pension
L/E Incentivised Late Retirement Larger State Pensions available to people who voluntarily increase takeup age for state pension
L/E Time-Extension - voluntary NI - UK workers People based in Ireland who have a work history of 3+ years in the UK may be eligible for both IE and UK state pension
S Digitisation of banks The three Irish retail banks (AIB, BOI, PTSB) have shifted from relationship- to transaction-based interfaces. This leaves a proportion of customers excluded and unable to access authorative information for financial decision making
L/E Digitisation of banks Digital transition paper -> website -> app means customer has less information at key decision points
L/E Reduced payment friction 1-click (or no-click) payments
L BNPL Buy-now-pay-later offered even for very short-term items, eg food deliveries, with unclear terms and penalties for late payments
E Cost-of-living increases Pressure on those who can least afford it, then nudged into more expensive options - eg pre-pay meter electricity
S Longevity - wealth transfer The disparity between male () and female () average lifespans means women may gain from inheritance from parents and spouse(s!)
P Finlit focus OECD and EU are tracking financial literacy and Dept of Finance are firmly focussed on improving in Ireland
