Is FLIT women-only?

'Empowering women to Financial Security' hmm is that a bit exclusionary???

In Ireland, women experience a per-hour salary gap of 11.2% (2023), and average â‚¬120K less in retirement pension savings.

Household resource management is still (2024) primarily a female responsibility. This includes administration for other family members: oversight of childcare, educational, medical and care arrangements, for younger AND older dependants. This can mean Women's personal needs being de-prioritised.

Hormonal influences on womens lives and lifecycles must also be acknowledged. Monthly periods may impact energy and mood. Postpartum (after birth), peri-menopause, menopausal hormonal changes similarly can affect cognitive and physical capabilities. (After menopause all bets are off, women often get a second wind, particularly once family demands reduce!)

The FLIT approach is not so much that men are excluded, rather than women are INCLUDED and prioritised. ('Gender-neutral' approaches just don't cut the mustard) This impacts:

  • FLIT presentation, tone and content
  • Content - structured as short lessons
  • 'Coaching' rather than prescriptive approach
  • Personalisation - tailored content
  • Lifestage approach - changing priorities, at different points in time
  • Task tracker
  • Community/sharing emphasis
