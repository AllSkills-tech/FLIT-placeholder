Feasibility study funding

Tuesday was a good-news day. I received word that my local enterprise office (LEO) will part-fund a FLIT feasibility study.

This works on a matched-funding basis, where I pay upfront, and once the feasibility study is completed, I can reclaim half the costs under authorised categories. I aimed high with the costings, and didn't get everything I'd asked for, but it feels like validation and support.

One key recommendation is to produce a marketing report.

Other activities planned for feasibility study are:

Identify stakeholders: people who would use FLIT, who would pay for FLIT, who would benefit from FLIT's existence.

Craft detailed customer persona(s) for site users - narrow reach which can later broaden.

What features are valuable to customers:

  • Financial guidance targeted by lifestage/ decision point?
  • Create & track personalised task lists?
  • Simplifying processes which require several visits to public websites?
  • A financial healthcheck?
  • Confidence-building tools?
  • A fraud-recognition game?

Develop FLIT V1 based on the above
